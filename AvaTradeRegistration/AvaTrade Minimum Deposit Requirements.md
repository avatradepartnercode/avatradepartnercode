## What Can You Do With $100?

Honestly? Not much comfortably.

At $100 account balance with typical leverage (say, 30:1 for forex in regulated regions), you can control about $3,000 in position size. One mini lot of EUR/USD moves about $1 per pip. A 100-pip loss wipes out your account.

$100 is enough to:
- Learn the platform interface
- Practice with real money psychology
- Test execution speeds
- Make a few small trades

It's not enough to trade seriously or develop a sustainable strategy with proper risk management.

## Practical Minimums

What traders actually recommend:

**$500-1,000**: Enough to trade micro positions with reasonable risk management. You won't get rich, but you can learn without blowing up immediately.

**$1,000-5,000**: Comfortable starting range for most retail traders. Allows proper position sizing across multiple trades.

**$5,000+**: Where things start getting serious. Enough capital to diversify, handle drawdowns, and trade with proper risk parameters.

The "right" amount depends entirely on your strategy, risk tolerance, and trading goals.

## The Bonus Math

Is it worth depositing $1,000 just for the bonus?

$1,000 deposit gets you $200 bonus (20%). That's meaningful.

But if you only have $500 comfortably available, stretching to $1,000 for an extra $200 isn't smart. You'd be overcommitting to get bonus credit you might never unlock.

The bonus is nice to have. It's not worth straining your finances.

## Currency Considerations

AvaTrade accepts deposits in multiple currencies. The minimums convert at current exchange rates.

If you're depositing in EUR, GBP, AUD, or another currency, check the equivalent amount. $100 USD is roughly €92 or £79, but rates fluctuate.

Depositing in your local currency avoids conversion fees on the way in. Just watch the conversion when you withdraw.

## No Deposit Required?

AvaTrade doesn't offer a no-deposit bonus like some competitors. You need to fund your account to trade.

They do offer a demo account with virtual funds. That requires no deposit and lets you practice on live market conditions. It's a good option if you're not ready to commit real money.

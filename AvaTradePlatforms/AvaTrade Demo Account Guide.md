## What's Different from Live

**No emotional weight.** Losing $5,000 in demo feels like nothing. Losing $5,000 of real money feels very different. This psychological gap is the main limitation of demo trading.

**Execution might be slightly faster.** Demo servers don't have the same load as live servers. You might notice marginally quicker fills in demo.

**No slippage simulation.** Demo fills at requested price. Live trading during volatility may experience slippage.

**No bonus or real costs.** Demo can't simulate the mental pressure of trading with bonus funds you want to unlock.

## How to Use Demo Productively

Most people waste their demo accounts. They click random buttons, blow up the virtual balance, and learn nothing. Here's a better approach:

**Treat it like real money.** Pretend the virtual balance is your actual savings. Trade the size you'd actually trade. Feel the decisions.

**Test your strategy.** If you have a trading plan, demo is where you prove it works before risking capital.

**Learn the platform.** Demo is perfect for figuring out where buttons are, how orders work, what happens when you modify positions.

**Practice execution.** Get comfortable placing stops, adjusting take profits, closing partial positions. Mechanical skills transfer to live trading.

**Time yourself.** How quickly can you execute a trade from idea to order? Demo lets you build muscle memory.

## Common Demo Mistakes

**Trading too big.** Starting with $100,000 virtual and trading full lots. That's not realistic if you'll deposit $1,000 live.

**Ignoring losses.** "It's just demo" leads to sloppy habits. Those habits carry over to real trading.

**Staying too long.** Demo is training wheels. At some point you need to go live (even with small size) to experience real trading psychology.

**Switching strategies constantly.** Demo is for testing one approach thoroughly, not jumping between systems every day.
